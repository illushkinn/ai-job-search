# Writing Style Guide — Illya Grytsyk

## Critical Rules

1. **NO em-dashes (--).** Use commas, periods, or restructure the sentence instead.
2. **NO cliches or filler phrases.** Cut: "I am passionate about", "I believe I would be a great fit", "leverage my skills", "hit the ground running", "drive results", "synergies".
3. **NO generic buzzwords** without concrete backing. Every claim must be supported by a specific example or fact.
4. **NO apologetic or overly humble language.** Not "I think I could contribute" but "I bring X, demonstrated by Y."
5. **NO unverified company claims.** Every company-specific statement must be independently verified.
6. **Reframe emphasis, not substance.** Apply the interview backtrack test.

## Tone
- **Warm but direct.** Friendly and approachable, but confident without arrogance.
- **Conversational professional.** Not stiff corporate-speak, not casual chat.
- **First person, active voice** (Spanish CVs). **Direct verbs, no "I"** (English CVs).
- **Demonstrate, don't state.** Instead of "I am a team player", write a specific example.

## Illya-Specific Tone Notes

### Spanish CV (cv-illya-ES)
- **Voseo rioplatense** in personal narratives, not formal "usted"
- Active first-person: "Diseñé", "Coordiné", "Construí", "Evalué", "Expuse"
- Months in lowercase: abril, marzo, enero
- Metrics-driven: concrete numbers contextualized
- Honest about production gap: "construyo prototipos de productos digitales y trabajo con clientes reales"
- Section title: "SOBRE MÍ" (not "PERFIL PROFESIONAL")

### English CV (cv-illya-EN)
- Direct verbs, no "I" at start of bullets: "Built", "Integrated", "Flagged", "Created"
- Adapt tone to role: more corporate for consultancies, more creative for agencies
- Same honest framing: "active transition toward Business Analysis and Product Engineering"

## Application Headline (Best Practice)

Formula: **[Title/specialty] + [relevant keyword from the job posting]**

**Bad examples:**
- "Application for Business Analyst Position"
- "Project Manager seeking new role"

**Good examples:**
- "Project Manager + AI Product — transitioning toward Business Analysis"
- "AI Product Strategist — Consumer Psychology & Agentic Systems"
- "Business Analyst with AI Evaluation Experience"

## Scannable Structure
- Use descriptive subheadings
- Include industry-specific keywords where natural
- Write concisely — eliminate filler language
- One page cover letter (hard rule)

## Forward-Looking Framing
The cover letter is **not a CV repetition**. Focus on:
- Tasks you can solve for the employer
- Your approach: methods, tools, knowledge you'll bring
- What positive outcomes the employer can expect
- Use 1-2 brief past examples only to back up forward-looking claims

## Cover Letter Structure

### Opening Paragraph
- State the role and why you're writing (1 sentence)
- Immediately connect background to the role (1-2 sentences)
- Specific to this company/role, not a template opener

### Body Paragraphs — Task-Solving Focus
- Lead with the most relevant experience for this specific role
- Frame content around which of their tasks you can solve and how
- Describe your approach: methods, tools, knowledge you'll bring
- Use bullet lists for concrete skills/achievements (3-5 bullets)
- Each bullet specific and outcome-oriented
- Include at least one example showing initiative

### Motivation / Why This Company
- Explain why you're applying to THIS specific company
- Reference their recent work, values, or market position
- Focus on how you'll contribute to their goals

### Closing
- Brief, confident, forward-looking
- "I look forward to the opportunity to discuss..."
- No begging or over-enthusiasm

## Bullet Point Style
- Start with action verb
- Be specific: numbers, tools, outcomes
- Vary the structure

## Role-Specific Writing Guidance

### For AI/Agentic Roles
- Lead with AI evaluation experience (Outlier), AI product thinking (Pragma), and agent architecture interest
- Frame technical understanding as product-enabling, not deep engineering
- Use terms: "AI evaluation methodology", "bias detection", "agent workflow design", "prompt engineering"

### For Business Analysis / Product Roles
- Lead with client/project coordination, requirement definition, stakeholder communication
- Frame technical skills as bridge-building between business and engineering
- Use terms: "requirements gathering", "stakeholder management", "product discovery", "sprint planning"

### For Consultancy Roles (Globant, Accenture)
- Lead with client-facing project experience, cross-functional team coordination
- Emphasize AI integration expertise as differentiator
- Use terms: "digital transformation", "client delivery", "technology advisory", "AI adoption"

### For Creative Agency Roles
- Lead with The Brand Crew experience, speed of delivery, design systems
- Emphasize AI integration in creative workflow as efficiency driver
- Use terms: "creative technology", "design operations", "AI-assisted production"

### For NZ / International Roles
- Lead with multilingual capability (Spanish, Ukrainian, Russian, English)
- Mention UNDP translation volunteer work (UN affiliation carries weight)
- Frame adaptability to new cultures and markets
- Be upfront about NZ work & travel visa plans

## Multi-language Applications
- Spanish cover letters: voseo rioplatense, warm professional
- English cover letters: direct, confident, slightly more formal
- CV language: Spanish for LATAM roles, English for international/NZ roles
- Always match cover letter language to the posting language

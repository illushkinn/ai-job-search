# Behavioral Profile — Illya Grytsyk

## Overview
Illya's behavioral profile (SnapHunt) identifies them as a **Tenaz · Efficiente · Constructor de relaciones** pattern. High tolerance for uncertainty, strong execution drive, and natural relationship-building ability. Best fit for startups, creative+tech intersections, and environments that value strategic thinking paired with hands-on execution.

## Core Behavioral Drives

| Drive | Level | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Tenacidad | High | Persistent problem-solver, doesn't give up on challenges, sees obstacles as puzzles to solve |
| Eficiencia | High | Optimizes for leverage, hates wasted effort, always looking for the most impactful path |
| Relaciones | High | Builds trust-based relationships naturally, communicates across disciplines, collaborates effectively |
| Autonomía | High | Works best with ownership and freedom, uncomfortable with micro-management |
| Curiosidad | High | Constant learner, explores tools and frameworks independently, applies new knowledge immediately |

## Strongest Behaviors
- **Persistencia estratégica:** Sticks with problems until solved, but knowing when to pivot (demonstrated by closing Stock para tu Negocio to pursue programming when the opportunity cost was right)
- **Comunicación multidisciplinaria:** Can talk to clients, designers, developers, and executives in their own language — bridges gaps between functions
- **Ejecución con criterio:** Doesn't just execute; thinks about WHY before doing, then executes with purpose

## How You Work Best
- **Alta incertidumbre:** Thrives in environments where the path isn't fully mapped — startups, new initiatives, innovation projects
- **Autonomía con propósito:** Needs ownership of outcomes, not just tasks. Wants to understand the "why" behind the work
- **Colaboración cross-functional:** Best when working across disciplines (design + tech + business), not siloed into one function
- **Feedback directo:** Prefers honest, direct communication over polished corporate diplomacy
- **Aprendizaje continuo:** Needs roles that involve learning new tools and approaches, not repetitive execution

## Growth Areas (frame positively in applications)

- "Startup environment" / "high autonomy" / "ownership"
- "Cross-functional collaboration" / "wear many hats"
- "Innovation" / "new initiatives" / "build from scratch"
- "Strategic thinking" / "product sense" / "business acumen"

**Potential friction (flag, not deal-breaker):**

- "Individual contributor" without strategic input (needs context, not just tasks)

## Management Style Preferences
- **Works best with:** Leaders who provide context and autonomy, not micromanagement. Prefers outcome-based management over activity-tracking
- **Doesn't work:** Rigid hierarchies, command-and-control, "because that's how we've always done it"
